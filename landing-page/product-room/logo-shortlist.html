Three marks. The rest is a system.

Inset Focus, Continuous Pane, and Split Focus are the strongest candidates from the first round. This page separates symbol, typeface, and color so each decision can be judged on its own.

All marks are tested in one color first. Accent is treated as an active state, not structural support.

The symbol shortlist.

Each mark has a different center of gravity. Inset Focus is iconic, Continuous Pane is ownable, and Split Focus explains the product most directly.

Inset Focus

iconic

Four outer corners frame one active surface. It has the clearest silhouette and survives the smallest product slots.

Continuous Pane

ownable

One route forms the rail, pane, and active task. It carries the most motion and has the most distinctive profile.

Split Focus

literal

One rounded cockpit contains a rail and two work surfaces. It communicates Panes before the name appears.

Six voices for the same name.

Every typeface is paired with all three marks. This keeps a good symbol from rescuing a weak wordmark and makes the differences in tone easier to see.

Color should behave like a signal.

The accent occupies only the active node or surface. Violet and mineral blue sit most naturally in the new landing page. Coral stays warmer and more urgent. Monochrome proves the marks can stand without color.

Three complete identity directions.

These are not final recommendations. They show how the same product can feel calm, native to the interface, or more explicitly technical by changing the combination rather than redesigning every part.
